Snorkelling

Welcome to the Yasawa Islands, where crystal-clear waters and vibrant coral reefs await adventures seeking an unforgettable snorkelling experience. Dive into the azure depths and discover a world teeming with life and color. Snorkelling is a popular activity in Yasawa Island. Snorkeling is a recreational activity that involves diving into the water and exploring the underwater world. Snorkeling is a popular activity for both beginners and experienced divers. Shark Feeding

There are very few places in the world where you can jump in the water with tiger sharks, bull sharks, tawny nurse sharks and lemon tip sharks without a cage! Get uninterrupted views of these majestic creatures on a shark scuba diving trip available with the Barefoot Kuata Resort. The team at Barefoot feed the sharks as part of local conservation efforts, while you watch the mesmerising show right in front of your eyes. If scuba diving with big sharks is out of your comfort zone (or budget), then snorkelling with reef sharks is another amazing experience available at Barefoot Kuata, Naqalia Lodge and homestays on Wayalailai Island.

Sawa-i-lau (The Heart Of Yasawa Islands

As mentioned before, Yasawa is also well known for Large underwater cave with misterious petroglyphs. One of the well known cave in Yasawa is the Sawa-i-Lau cave, Sawa-i-Lau's secret attraction is hidden within the bowels of its domed mountain. Two caverns, filled with turquoise seawater pools that are fed by the tides. Daylight floods the first (and larger) cave from an opening along its southern ceiling and wall. You’ll need a guide (and a bit of courage) to lead you through the secretive underwater entrance to the second, smaller cavern. According to the guides, it’s only when you’ve entered this dark, inner sanctum that you’ve visited the true ‘heart of the Yasawas.
